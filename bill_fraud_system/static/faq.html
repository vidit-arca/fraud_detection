Frequently Asked Questions

Understanding how our AI detects document fraud

What is ELA (Error Level Analysis)?
ELA stands for Error Level Analysis. It's a forensic technique that identifies areas of an image that have been compressed at different levels. When someone digitally edits a document (like pasting a new number over an old one), the pasted section often has a different "compression quality" than the background. Our system detects these inconsistencies to spot tampering that is invisible to the naked eye.
What does "Deep Feature Analysis" mean?
We use advanced AI (EfficientNet) to examine the structure and layout of the document. The model looks at fonts, spacing, logo placement, and line alignment. If a document claims to be a medical bill but uses fonts or layouts that don't match known genuine examples, the deep feature analysis flags it as a "structural anomaly."
Why was my genuine bill flagged as "Tampered"?
Our model effectively learns what "normal" bills look like from a specific set of training data (e.g., bills from specific hospitals). If you upload a genuine bill from a new hospital or with a significantly different layout that the model hasn't seen before, it may be flagged as an anomaly. This is a safety precaution—any document that significantly deviates from the known standard is marked for review.
What file types are supported?
We support JPG, PNG, and PDF files. PDF documents are automatically converted to high-resolution images on our secure server before analysis. For best results, use original digital files or high-quality scans rather than photos of screens.
What does "Inconsistent Noise Pattern" mean?
Every digital image has a natural "grain" or noise pattern created by the camera sensor or scanner. When an image is digitally manipulated, this noise pattern is often disrupted or smoothed out in the edited areas. Our system detects these subtle interruptions in the noise texture to identify forged regions.
